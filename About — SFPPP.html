San Francisco Peninsula People Power (SFPPP) is a non-partisan, grassroots civil rights organization, organized as a result of a nationwide event by the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) in March 2017. The People Power initiative was created to help volunteers  build and strengthen local communities that affirm our American values of respect, equality, and solidarity. Our membership is located in San Mateo and Santa Clara Counties.

The ACLU blog has more information about the launch of People Power, the Freedom Cities campaign, and the Let People Vote campaign.

Our Meetings

SFPPP meets the first Thursday of every month (subject to change around major holidays) in San Mateo County. You get more detail by signing up to attend a meeting at the People Power website. Each monthly meeting is posted to the events list approximately three weeks in advance.

Attend a Meeting

We welcome anyone interested in defending civil rights locally and nationally.

Contact SFPPP

If you have questions, would like more information about our group, or just want to bring something to our attention, please email us at sfpeninsulapp@gmail.com.

Support SFPPP

We are an all-volunteer organization, and we do incur expenses as we work to defend civil rights locally. If you'd like to support our work, please email us at sfpeninsulapp@gmail.com and we will put you in touch with the right person. Your support helps us create educational materials, meet our travel expenses, host this volunteer-created website, and other incidental costs.
